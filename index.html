About

I'm Alia, an Egyptian creative technologist and researcher based between Brooklyn and Cairo. With a background in computer science and interactive media, I'm now a political science PhD student at NYU. My research interests are in the relationships between social media and political knowledge, participation, and public opinion.

I build online interactive experiences that make the impact of technology on society more accessible and engaging for broad audiences. These include Survival of the Best Fit, an educational game on AI bias built by Jihyun Kim, Miha Klasinc, and Gabor Csapo with funding and support from Mozilla's Creative Media Award.

Alongside Lujain Ibrahim, I co-built Multiplicity, an online curation of geographically diverse youth-centric articles on the sociopolitical and personal impacts of the internet. As grantees of another Mozilla Creative Media Award, Lujain and I are now building "The Algorithm" (live Jan 2024), an interactive simulation that demystifies how content is recommended on our algorithmically curated, video-based social media feeds (e.g., TikTok, Instagram Reels).

For more info, you can check my LinkedIn. Feel free to contact me at alia.elkattan@nyu.edu or find me on Twitter, Instagram, GitHub, and/or Bluesky.
